# Revenue experiment

This is a controlled learning plan, not a revenue guarantee. The purpose is to determine whether better public evidence, targeting, personalization, timing, and follow-up discipline produce legitimate conversations and paid work at low volume.

## Initial hypothesis

For a narrow clinic campaign, an owner-approved email that cites one verifiable website issue and offers one bounded implementation outline will produce more useful replies than generic agency outreach, without requiring high send volume or deceptive tracking.

The first experiment should use one country and one niche. The deterministic test campaign is not a prospect source and must never contact its reserved fixture domain.

## Offer ladder

| Offer | Purpose | Payment boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Diagnostic | Explain the selected evidence-backed issue and practical remediation | Owner approves scope and price before checkout |
| Implementation sprint | Deliver only the approved correction or implementation brief | No site modification without written authorization and access |
| Monitoring | Optional recurring verification after delivery | Separate recurring consent and verified provider payment |

Do not use fake discounts, unsupported savings, revenue projections, ranking promises, patient-growth claims, or fabricated urgency.

## Test phases

### Phase 0 — deterministic acceptance

Run `npm run acceptance`. Required result: every transition passes in test mode; one simulated email, one simulated reply, one signed test webhook, one paid test order, and one delivery task; no external network or customer action.

### Phase 1 — dry-run discovery and review

- Select one narrow campaign and city-sized discovery areas.
- Preview discovery before import.
- Review the first 25 public websites and all rejection reasons.
- Audit and draft no more than 25 qualified prospects.
- Manually inspect every selected issue, contact source, and draft.
- Record false evidence, wrong niche, contact-risk, duplicate, and weak-copy rates.

Pass only if evidence and contact admission are consistently correct. A low number of prospects is not a reason to weaken gates.

### Phase 2 — authenticated provider tests

- Connect one owner-controlled Gmail test inbox without enabling live outreach.
- Verify threading, unsubscribe, reply stopping, bounce, complaint, and sender pause using controlled test mailboxes.
- Complete one Lemon Squeezy test-mode checkout and webhook.
- Confirm the dashboard and delivery task match provider records.

### Phase 3 — tiny owner-observed pilot

This phase requires owner authorization, provider authentication, jurisdiction review, sender-domain authentication, and a separate deliberate live activation. Start with manual approval and 10 or fewer initial messages. Do not auto-negotiate, auto-send proposals, or auto-request payment.

Stop the pilot immediately for a complaint, broken unsubscribe, unexpected duplicate, provider ambiguity, evidence error, or material contact-quality issue.

## Measurements

Track by campaign, country, niche, evidence type, subject variant, message variant, CTA, recipient role, UTC send-time bucket, and inbox:

- discovered and qualified;
- draft approved;
- sent and delivered when the provider supplies a credible signal;
- bounced and unsubscribed;
- replied, positive reply, and meeting requested;
- proposal sent and checkout sent;
- verified paid and net revenue by currency;
- delivery completed.

Do not use open pixels or deceptive open tracking. Optimize around replies and commercial outcomes.

## Decision rules

- Require at least 20 observations per variant before evaluating a comparison.
- Retain absolute counts, denominators, and historical evaluations.
- Treat low-volume results as inconclusive, not as proof of a winner.
- Never increase sending caps automatically.
- Recommendations require owner approval and may not mutate campaign configuration by themselves.
- Prefer evidence accuracy, positive-reply quality, low complaint/unsubscribe rates, verified payment, and completed delivery over raw send volume.

## Weekly owner review

1. Activate the global kill switch before investigating any safety anomaly.
2. Review complaints, bounces, unsubscribes, ambiguous sends, and unsupported-evidence reports first.
3. Inspect positive replies and delivery obligations.
4. Compare funnel outcomes only when minimum sample sizes are met.
5. Approve, reject, or defer each recommendation with a written reason.
6. Keep caps unchanged unless provider health and owner-reviewed outcomes justify a deliberate change.

## Success and failure

Success means a repeatable, respectful path from credible public evidence to owner-approved conversations, verified payment, and delivered scope. Failure includes unsafe targeting, low evidence fidelity, weak replies, high negative signals, poor fulfillment economics, or an operational burden that conflicts with the owner’s study time. Either result is useful learning; neither guarantees future income.
